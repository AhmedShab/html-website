Final Fantasy VII

Final Fantasy VII is the seventh main installment in the Final Fantasy series, developed and published by Squaresoft. It was released in January 1997 for the PlayStation, and later re-released for several platforms including Microsoft Windows, various PlayStation consoles, iOS, Android, Xbox One, and Nintendo Switch. It is also included on the PlayStation Classic mini console.

The game was directed by Yoshinori Kitase, produced by Hironobu Sakaguchi, with music by Nobuo Uematsu, and character designs by Tetsuya Nomura. It was notable for being the first in the series to feature three-dimensional graphics, pre-rendered backgrounds, and numerous full-motion videos. It was also the first main series game released in Europe and Oceania.

Story

The story follows Cloud Strife, a mercenary who joins the eco-terrorist group AVALANCHE, led by Barret Wallace, to fight against the mega-corporation Shinra Electric Power Company. Initially indifferent, Cloud's journey becomes one of personal growth and collective struggle, ultimately joining forces with others to save the planet from Shinra and the antagonist Sephiroth.

Gameplay

Departing from previous titles, Final Fantasy VII retains the Active Time Battle system but introduces a three-member party system instead of four. The game features the Materia system for ability customization and the Limit system for unique combat skills. Additionally, it introduces a variety of minigames playable in the Gold Saucer amusement park.

Impact and Legacy

With over 14.1 million units sold, Final Fantasy VII is the best-selling game in the series and is considered one of the most influential RPGs ever. Its story, characters, and soundtrack have left a lasting impact on gaming and popular culture, appearing frequently on lists of the greatest games of all time.

Sequels and Spinoffs

The game's success spawned several prequels, sequels, and spinoffs, known as the Compilation of Final Fantasy VII. This includes the Final Fantasy VII Remake project. On January 31, 2023, the 26th anniversary of its release, January 31 was officially registered as "Final Fantasy VII Day" in Japan.

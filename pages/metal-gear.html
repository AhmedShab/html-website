Metal Gear Series

Metal Gear (Japanese: メタルギア, Hepburn: Metaru Gia) is a franchise of stealth games created by Hideo Kojima and developed and published by Konami. The first game, titled Metal Gear, was released in 1987 for MSX home computers.

Gameplay

Players often assume the role of a special forces operative, commonly either Solid Snake or Big Boss, tasked with finding the superweapon "Metal Gear"—a bipedal walking tank capable of launching nuclear weapons.

Development and Impact

The franchise debuted with the 1987 game and has since expanded through several sequels on multiple gaming consoles, exploring deeper plots and character development. Metal Gear Solid, the third game released for PlayStation, shifted to 3D graphics and significantly boosted the series' international acclaim.

Metal Gear is credited with pioneering stealth video games and "cinematic video games," featuring notable traits such as cinematic cutscenes, intricate storylines, and fourth-wall humor. Themes explored include cyberpunk and dystopian settings, political and philosophical ideologies, along with references to Hollywood films.

Legacy and Popularity

With over 61 million units sold by December 2023, the Metal Gear series has been critically acclaimed and awarded, influencing a wide range of media, including comics, novels, and drama CDs. Characters like Solid Snake have appeared in other popular video games, including the Super Smash Bros. series, Ape Escape 3, LittleBigPlanet, and Fortnite.

Media and Adaptations

Besides games, Metal Gear has expanded into other media forms like comics and novels, helping to solidify its position as a significant cultural phenomenon in gaming history.
